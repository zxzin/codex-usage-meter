# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Codex users who want to see short-window and weekly usage without repeatedly opening account menus. They keep Token Meter visible beside their work and expect it to stay quiet, legible, and responsive to real activity.

## Product Purpose

Token Meter turns Codex quota and recent token burn into a compact always-on-top desktop signal. Success means the remaining allowance is readable at a glance, the bee motion reflects activity smoothly, and setup does not expose credentials or create another account.

## Brand Personality

Playful, precise, compact. The honeycomb and bee make a technical limit approachable, while the data behavior remains trustworthy and restrained.

## Anti-references

- Generic monitoring dashboards with decorative charts and oversized cards.
- Marketing artwork presented as if it were the real application.
- Noisy game HUDs that make the quota harder to read.
- Demo or review flows that require sharing real Codex credentials.

## Design Principles

- Show the real product first: store media and review evidence must use the shipped interface.
- Preserve data trust: quota state, motion, and fallback behavior must reflect a documented source.
- Keep the widget glanceable: the two quota windows remain the primary visual signal.
- Let motion explain activity: bees accelerate and widen their orbit smoothly without interrupting reading.
- Make review and setup safe: sample data must never contain reusable credentials.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Maintain readable contrast at small widget sizes, preserve meaning without relying on color alone, avoid flashing or abrupt motion, and respect reduced-motion preferences in presentation surfaces.
